For a coordinated long-term response to epidemic threats, including COVID-19, Canadian researchers should coordinate their data collection and data governance practices with those of established international consortia to facilitate efficient data deposit (Council of Canadian Academies 2015).
The COVID-19 pandemic has lain bare the fault lines in Canada’s health data regulation.
Challenges faced in sharing health data to respond to the COVID-19 epidemic demonstrate how Canada’s duplicative privacy legislation can create considerable barriers for health data sharing in Canada with minimal gains in personal privacy.
