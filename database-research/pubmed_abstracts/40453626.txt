Composite hemangioendothelioma (CHE) is a rare and enigmatic vascular neoplasm that exhibits complex and heterogeneous characteristics.
This comprehensive review explores the unique nature of Composite hemangioendothelioma (CHE), with a particular focus on its occurrence at the base of the tongue.
Composite hemangioendothelioma (CHE) falls within the category of locally aggressive, rarely metastasizing vascular neoplasms.
It is characterized by the presence of histologically distinct components, demonstrating features of benign hemangioma and malignant angiosarcoma.
A thorough literature review of pubmed published articles was done.
