Cervical cancer is a significant global health concern, especially in low- and middle-income countries with limited access to preventive healthcare.
India's vast rural population amplifies the challenge, demanding immediate action.
Despite advancements, cervical cancer remains prevalent among underserved rural communities, hindered by barriers to Human Papillomavirus (HPV) screening uptake, including socioeconomic and financial constraints.
This study aims to evaluate the economic challenges encountered by rural women when accessing HPV screening.
