The association between skeletal muscle mass and postoperative morbidity in cancer patients has been demonstrated, but the results are not conclusive.
The study aims to determine the predictive efficacy of rectus abdominis muscle (RAM) and psoas major muscle (PMM) thickness and other factors such as age, American Society of Anesthesiologists (ASA) score, operation duration, obesity, preoperative inflammatory markers, and pathological findings for postoperative morbidity.
