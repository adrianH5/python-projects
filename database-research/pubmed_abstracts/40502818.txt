Radiation therapy for locally-advanced cervical cancer consists of external beam radiation therapy and brachytherapy.
Image-guided brachytherapy and hybrid intracavitary and interstitial brachytherapy treatments (HBT) can potentially improve outcomes; however, current literature describes the procedure requiring significant resources, limiting widespread use.
Here is described a single institutional experience with CT-guided interstitial needle placement in a typical freestanding outpatient setting.
