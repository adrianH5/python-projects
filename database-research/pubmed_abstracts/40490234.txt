the most common halo-tolerant plant, is rich in polyphenolic compounds such as phenols, flavonoids, saponins, alkaloids, and steroids.
Avicennia marina exhibits significant therapeutic potential, including antioxidants, antiviral, immunosuppressive, and anticancer properties.
Numerous halo-tolerant plant metabolites have been examined for their anti-cancer effects; nevertheless, their efficacy against Glioblastoma multiforme (GBM), an aggressive malignant neoplasm, remains poorly understood.
