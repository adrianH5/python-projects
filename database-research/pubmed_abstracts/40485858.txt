Inguinal lymph node dissection (ILND) is crucial for staging and treating several malignancies, but it is often associated with significant morbidity due to postoperative complications.
This case series aimed to evaluate the safety and feasibility of using the superficial circumflex iliac artery perforator (L-SCIP) flap, enriched with lymphatic tissue, following ILND and examine its possible effect in preventing these complications.
