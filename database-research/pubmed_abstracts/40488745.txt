Although photodynamic diagnosis (PDD) using 5-aminolevulinic acid (ALA) is a highly a sensitive and excellent method for detecting nonmuscle-invasive bladder cancer, false-positive rates should not be overlooked.
One of the factors causing false-positive results is inflammation.
Currently, the relationship between pyuria and PDD accuracy remains unclear.
Therefore, this study aimed to clarify the influence of preoperative pyuria on the diagnostic accuracy of PDD.
