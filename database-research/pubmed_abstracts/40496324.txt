Haematological malignancies (HMs) account for approximately 10% of all malignancies in sub-Saharan Africa (SSA), and their incidence is rapidly increasing.
Successful management of these malignancies depends on early presentation, diagnosis and prompt initiation of treatment.
However, in SSA and many low and middle-income countries, several barriers hinder the effective management of these malignancies.
This case report of a patient with acute promyelocytic anaemia highlights the challenges in managing HM.
