This comment explores the pivotal role of qualitative research for expanding knowledge and practice-and filling in gaps-around protecting patients with cancer from compounding disasters.
Climate-related hazards are creating accelerating threats to population health, particularly in the realm of exposures to perilous heat and extreme weather events.
Patients with cancer, including those recently diagnosed, currently undergoing treatment, or in survivorship, experience elevated risks due to their compromised health status and vulnerability to cancer care disruptions during disasters.
Patients with cancer require customized support for performing both general and cancer care-specific disaster preparedness actions.
