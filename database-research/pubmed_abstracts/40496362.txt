As a member of the chaperonin-containing tailless complex polypeptide 1 (TCP1) complex, which plays a pivotal role in ensuring the accurate folding of numerous proteins, chaperonin-containing TCP1 subunit 6A (CCT6A) participates in various physiological and pathological processes.
However, its effects on cell death and cancer therapy and the underlying mechanisms need further exploration in colorectal cancer (CRC) cells.
