Self-testicular examination is a simple, cost-effective, and noninvasive method for early detection.
In recent years, peer education has gained prominence as a complementary approach to traditional testicular self-examination (TSE) instruction.
This study investigates the impact of peer education on health responsibility, health beliefs, knowledge, and practices related to the early detection of testicular cancer among university students.
This randomized controlled trial was conducted between October 2023 and June 2024, involving 280 male students (140 in the experimental group and 140 in the control group).
Data were collected using a sociodemographic questionnaire, the Testicular Cancer and Self-Examination Knowledge Test, and a Health Responsibility and Health Belief Model questionnaire at baseline and 6 months post-intervention.
