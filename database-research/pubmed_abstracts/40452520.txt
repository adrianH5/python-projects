Pleurocutaneous fistula is a pathological communication of subcutaneous tissue with the pleural cavity, and can occur as a result of infectious, malignant processes and iatrogenic procedures.
Colopleural fistula is rare and is mainly caused by processes in the abdomen.
The appearance of empyema is usually described as a complication of colopleural fistulas that are the result of pathological processes in the abdomen.
