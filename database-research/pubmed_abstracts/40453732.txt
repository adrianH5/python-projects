Cancer has emerged as a leading cause of death worldwide, surpassing HIV/AIDS, tuberculosis, and malaria combined.
Nigeria's cases and deaths continue to increase every year due to the high cancer burden that affects the country.
There were 269,109 new cancer cases registered in 2022, with breast and prostate cancer being the most prevalent.
Despite improvements made, several problems related to the diagnosis and treatment of cancer across the nation remain.
This paper reviews these issues while discussing what is being done to establish an efficient system for managing cancer care in Nigeria.
