We report a 31-year-old woman with a history of low-grade, undifferentiated spindle cell sarcoma of the hand diagnosed at 10 years of age and subsequently amputated.
The patient remained under surveillance without recurrence for over 20 years before she presented with multiple new masses in the skin of the head and neck.
Biopsies confirmed recurrent sarcoma.
Imaging studies revealed metastases within the lungs and peri-pancreatic tissue.
