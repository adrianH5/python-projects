Patients with head and neck squamous cell carcinoma (HNSCC) suffer from severe morbidity and mortality.
Immunotherapy represents a novel promising treatment option.
Therefore, a better understanding of the immune niche is needed.
This study focuses on the spatial distribution and prognostic value of different T cell subtypes in 84 HNSCC specimens as well as chemokine and cytokine levels associated with spatial T cell infiltration.
